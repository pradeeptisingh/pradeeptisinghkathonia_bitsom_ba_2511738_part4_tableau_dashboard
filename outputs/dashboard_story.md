# Executive Dashboard Story
## Retail Sales Performance: 2024–2025

---

## Executive Summary

Over the two-year period from January 2024 to December 2025, the business generated **₹217 million in total sales** and **₹33.3 million in total profit**, yielding an overall profit margin of **15.3%**. The portfolio spans three product categories (Technology, Furniture, Office Supplies), four sales regions, three customer segments, and five campaign channels — serving customers across 19 Indian states.

The dashboard tells a clear story: **Technology is the engine. Furniture is the anchor dragging it down. Discounting is the hidden tax.** Leadership must act on these dynamics to protect and grow margin in the next planning cycle.

---

## What Is Performing Well

**Technology is the growth leader.** Representing 70.9% of total sales (₹153.9M) and 84.2% of total profit (₹28.0M), Technology products — led by Copiers, Accessories, Phones, and Machines — all deliver margins above 18%. This is the business's core profit engine and deserves continued investment in range expansion and marketing spend.

**The South region leads in absolute terms.** With ₹64.7M in sales and a 15.44% margin, the South — anchored by Telangana, Karnataka, Kerala, and Tamil Nadu — is the most productive region. Strong performance here suggests mature channel relationships and effective local sales execution.

**Home Office buyers deliver the highest margins.** At 15.51%, the Home Office segment marginally outperforms Corporate and Consumer. As remote and hybrid work continues to normalize, this segment represents a long-term structural tailwind that should be cultivated with targeted loyalty programs.

**Seasonal peaks are reliable.** Sales consistently strengthen in July–August and October–November, signaling predictable demand cycles that can be planned around with inventory and campaign timing.

---

## What Is Underperforming

**Furniture is a margin problem.** Despite contributing ₹51.6M in sales, Furniture delivers only 6.89% overall margin — compared to 18.22% for Technology. Sub-categories like Tables (5.67%) and Bookcases (5.71%) are barely profitable and, in many individual orders, are operating at a loss. When compounded with the category's 7.67% return rate, Furniture is net-negative on a full unit economics basis in multiple scenarios.

**Discounting is out of control.** Orders carrying discounts of 21–35% average just ₹2,915 in profit — 78% less than undiscounted orders. High-discount orders are concentrated in Furniture and select Office Supplies sub-categories, suggesting that sales teams are using discounting as a crutch rather than value-based selling. This behavior is eroding margins systemically.

**Standard Class shipping underserves customers.** The most-used shipping mode averages 4.71 delivery days — far behind First Class (1.77 days) and Same Day (0.40 days). In a competitive retail environment, slow delivery risks customer dissatisfaction and repeat-purchase erosion, particularly for high-value orders.

---

## Risks Visible in the Data

**Furniture double-loss risk.** The simultaneous combination of the lowest margins (6.89%) and the highest return rate (7.67%) in Furniture creates a scenario where returned orders represent a certain loss after accounting for operational costs. This requires urgent corrective action.

**Discount escalation risk.** If the current discounting trajectory continues without guardrails, the business risks training customers and sales teams to expect deep discounts as standard practice — making it structurally harder to recover margins.

**Delivery delay risk in Standard Class.** Orders shipping via Standard Class with 6–7 day delivery windows are at risk of attracting negative reviews or returns. The return rate for slow-shipping orders appears higher than for faster modes.

**Campaign channel imbalance.** Heavy reliance on Organic channel (no paid acquisition) suggests the business may hit a growth ceiling without investing in paid or referral programs, particularly for scaling into new states.

---

## Opportunities Visible in the Data

**Expand Technology SKUs and marketing.** Given Technology's 18%+ margin across all four sub-categories, incremental investment in category growth generates outsized profit. A focused expansion into underpenetrated states in the East (Jharkhand, Assam, Bihar) could drive significant upside.

**Launch a referral program.** The Referral channel is currently underutilized. With high-trust, low-CAC characteristics, a structured referral incentive for existing Corporate and Home Office customers could unlock new customers at below-average acquisition cost.

**Upgrade high-value orders to faster shipping.** Same Day and First Class shipping correlate with higher average order values and profit per order. Proactively upgrading orders above ₹50,000 to First Class at no extra cost could improve customer satisfaction and loyalty without significant cost impact.

**Fix Furniture before cutting it.** Rather than immediately deprioritizing Furniture, there is an opportunity to improve its economics by capping discounts, improving product imagery/descriptions to reduce returns, and focusing on higher-margin sub-categories like Chairs (8.25%) and Furnishings (7.86%).

## Limitations of the Dashboard

- **No customer-level retention data.** The dashboard tracks orders but cannot distinguish between repeat customers and new customers, limiting churn analysis.
- **No cost-of-acquisition data.** Campaign channel performance cannot be evaluated on a true ROI basis without knowing spend per channel.
- **Return reasons are unknown.** The `return_flag` field indicates whether an item was returned but not why — limiting root cause analysis.
- **Geography limited to 19 states.** Several large Indian states (e.g., Maharashtra has Mumbai but may not fully represent rural markets) may have incomplete coverage.
- **Delivery days vs. actual SLA breach.** The dashboard shows delivery days but does not indicate whether each shipment breached its promised SLA window.

---

## Suggested Next Analysis

1. **Customer cohort analysis** – Track repeat purchase rates by segment and region to identify retention patterns.
2. **Return reason analysis** – Enrich return data with reason codes to enable targeted product and process fixes.
3. **Campaign channel ROI** – Integrate marketing spend data to calculate true cost-per-acquisition by channel.
4. **State-level growth opportunity map** – Identify high-potential states with current low penetration versus income/population potential.
5. **Pricing elasticity study** – Model how margin responds to discount levels at sub-category granularity to establish evidence-based discount thresholds.
